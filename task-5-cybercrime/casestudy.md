## Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity.

A common phishing attack usually happens in the following steps. First, the attacker sends a fake email or message that appears to be from a bank, company, or service provider. The message often creates urgency, such as claiming that the user’s account will be blocked. Second, the message contains a malicious link that redirects the user to a fake website that looks real. Third, the user unknowingly enters their personal information on this fake site. Finally, the attacker collects this data and uses it for fraud or unauthorized access.

Students, working professionals, and elderly people are common targets because they may not always verify links or messages carefully. The consequences of phishing can be severe, including financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.

For example, a student receives an email claiming to be from a scholarship portal asking for login details. Without checking the authenticity, the student enters their credentials, which are then stolen and misused.

Phishing attacks can be prevented by verifying sources, avoiding suspicious links, and using strong security practices such as two-factor authentication.
